## Overview

Video Large Language Models (Vid-LLMs) have recently achieved strong performance in video understanding tasks such as video question answering, captioning, and multimodal reasoning. However, these models often generate outputs that are not faithfully grounded in the input video, a phenomenon known as **hallucination**. Compared with hallucination in text-only or image-based models, hallucination in video understanding is more complex due to temporal dynamics, motion interpretation, long-context dependencies, and event-level reasoning.

This survey provides a comprehensive review of hallucination in Video Large Language Models. We present a unified taxonomy of hallucination phenomena in video understanding and systematically organize existing mitigation techniques according to the failure mechanisms they address. The survey also highlights several promising research directions for building more reliable and trustworthy Vid-LLMs in the future.
